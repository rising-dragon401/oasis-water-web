**Purified Water (by Reverse Osmosis)**

Reverse osmosis (RO) purified water represents a technological triumph in providing clean, safe drinking water. Here's the lowdown on how RO purified water can affect our health:

Benefits of RO Purified Water:

- Optimal Hydration: Reverse osmosis water is free from impurities and dissolved solids, making it an excellent choice for staying hydrated without the concern of consuming unwanted substances.
- Taste Purity: The purification process strips away elements that might affect the taste, offering a clean and refreshing drinking experience.
- Detoxified Option: With contaminants like lead, arsenic, and nitrates being removed, it serves as a safer alternative to tap water in areas where such pollutants are a concern.

Potential Downsides to Consider:

- Mineral Deficiency: Essential minerals found in water are also removed during reverse osmosis. This might necessitate an additional mineral intake from other dietary sources.
- Acidity Levels: The lack of minerals can lead to a slightly more acidic pH level than is typical in natural water, which may affect sensitive individuals or those with specific health conditions.
- Environmental Cost: The process is water-intensive, potentially wasting two to three gallons for every gallon purified, and the filters and membranes require periodic replacement, adding to environmental waste.

In sum, reverse osmosis water is like a blank canvas—pristine and pure. It's an excellent medium for hydration, especially in areas where tap water quality is questionable, but just like a balanced diet, it's important to ensure you're getting your essential minerals from other sources.

**Sodium Bicarbonate**

Benefits:

- pH Balance: Sodium Bicarbonate is alkaline, and its addition to drinking water can raise the pH level. This can be beneficial for those seeking to balance their body's pH or alleviate symptoms caused by high acidity, such as acid reflux.
- Digestive Aid: In moderate amounts, Sodium Bicarbonate can help neutralize stomach acid, providing relief from indigestion and heartburn. It's a component of many over-the-counter antacids for this reason.
- Electrolyte Balance: It provides a source of sodium, which is an essential electrolyte in the body. Proper electrolyte balance is crucial for hydration, muscle function, and nerve signaling.

However, there are potential drawbacks to consider:

- Sodium Intake: Increased consumption of Sodium Bicarbonate can lead to higher sodium intake, which might not be suitable for individuals with high blood pressure or those on a sodium-restricted diet.
- Possible Overuse: Excessive use can lead to metabolic alkalosis, a condition where the body's pH becomes too alkaline, potentially causing symptoms like muscle weakness, twitching, or even seizures in severe cases.
- Drug Interactions: It can interact with certain medications, altering their absorption or effectiveness. It's important to consult with a healthcare provider if on prescription drugs before increasing Sodium Bicarbonate intake.

Sodium Bicarbonate in drinking water can offer some health benefits, particularly for digestive issues and maintaining a balanced internal pH. However, it's essential to use it judiciously to avoid the risks associated with high sodium consumption and potential health complications from overuse. As always, individual health conditions and dietary needs should be taken into account. Embracing the dual-edged nature of dipotassium phosphate in our drinking water, we recognize its beneficial embrace and its potential sting. This compound, more than just a simple ingredient, can influence our well-being, depending on its presence in our water supply. Let's unfold the narrative of Magnesium Sulfate and its double-edged sword upon human health.

The Tale of Magnesium Sulfate in Drinking Water:

- A Mineral Maestro: Magnesium Sulfate is a source of magnesium, a mineral maestro in the symphony of our bodily functions. It aids in over 300 enzymatic reactions, fortifying bones, and supporting nerve and muscle function.
- A Relaxation Respite: When present in water, it can be a relaxation agent, soothing muscles and providing a calming effect, akin to the tranquility one finds in a warm, Epsom salt bath.

However, striking a balance is key, as the tale of Magnesium Sulfate is not without its shadows:

- A Kidney's Burden: The kidneys bear the onus of filtering out excess magnesium; too grand a task can stress these vital organs, particularly in individuals with kidney disease.
- An Electrolyte Imbalance: Immoderate consumption can lead to an imbalance of electrolytes in the body, disrupting the harmonious rhythm of our internal processes.

In essence, like the delicate balance of a well-orchestrated ensemble, Magnesium Sulfate in our drinking water must be fine-tuned to our individual health compositions and needs. While it can sing a soothing melody of benefits, it's critical to listen to the underlying notes and ensure they harmonize with our body’s unique requirements.

**Calcium Chloride**

Calcium chloride, a compound you might recognize from its role in road deicing or as a firming agent in canned vegetables, is also a player in the water we drink. But like any character in the drama of our dietary intake, it wears both white and black hats depending on the scene. Let's set the stage for how this compound interacts with our body's intricate workings when it's a part of our drinking water.

- The Bone Benefactor: In the grand theater of human health, calcium is the lead actor for strong bones and teeth. When calcium chloride is present in water, it contributes to the daily calcium performance, ensuring that the skeletal framework remains robust and resilient against the test of time.
- Muscle Maestro: Like a conductor's baton guiding the orchestra, calcium is essential for the proper function of muscles. It helps in the contraction and relaxation phases, ensuring that each muscle note is hit with precision.
- Electrolyte Ensemble: In the human body's fluid symphony, electrolytes like calcium play a crucial role in maintaining the harmony of hydration. Calcium chloride in water can be a source of these electrolytes, tuning the body's fluid balance to a perfect pitch.
- Heart's Rhythm Section: Calcium's role in heart health cannot be understated—it's the rhythm section that keeps the beat steady and strong. Adequate calcium intake from all sources, including water, supports the heart in its tireless rhythm.

However, even the most beneficial elements can hit a sour note if the composition isn't balanced:

- The Salty Solo: Calcium chloride is saltier to the taste than its counterpart, calcium sulfate. This can be a discordant note for taste buds expecting the neutral taste of pure water, leading to a less than refreshing experience.
- Desiccation Dynamics: Calcium chloride has a hygroscopic nature, meaning it can attract and hold water molecules from its surroundings. In excess, this could potentially lead to a drying effect on the body, requiring one to be mindful of the balance of intake.
- The Kidney's Critique: While the kidneys are adept at maintaining mineral balance, an overabundance of calcium—especially in the presence of an underlying renal condition—can overtax these vital organs, causing a cacophony of health issues.
- Scale Accumulation: In the pipes and purviews of our water systems, calcium chloride can contribute to scale buildup. While not a direct health concern, it's a logistical aria that can affect water quality and appliance longevity.

As we navigate the waters of our daily consumption, it's essential to consider the sources and amounts of calcium chloride we're inviting in. While it can be a supporting actor contributing to the overall performance of our health, we must ensure it doesn't steal the show with potential overexposure. It's all about maintaining a harmonious balance in the liquid libretto of life.

# Terms and Definitions

- Statement of quality: the maximum legal limits for a substance in bottled water. The substances include pesticides, radiological contaminants, microbiological contaminants, organic chemicals, inorganic chemicals and others. The standards are established by the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA)
- Maximum contaminant level (MCL): maximum level of a contaminant allowed in public drinking water
- Primary drinking water standards (PDWS): maximum feasable protection of public health. Determined by the combination of ecomonimc and technological feasibility.
- Public Health Goal (PHG): the level of a contaminant in drinking water below which there is no known or expected risk to health. PHGs are set by the California Environmental Protection Agency.
