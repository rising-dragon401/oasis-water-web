## Essentia Overview

**Oaisys score:** 75 / 100

**Owner and Manufacturer**: Fiji Water Company.

**Treatment process**: This water is naturally filtered and replenished by rainfall and volcanic activity in the area, which contributes to its mineral content and taste. The water is botled at source
by pumping water through a sealed deliver system free of human contact. The water is filtered to remove any particle matter, micron-filtered to remove microbiological particles,
and ultra violet light is applied to ensure disenfection.

**Source**: artesian aquifer in the Yaqara Valley of Fiji's main island, Viti Levu.

**pH level**: 7.7

**Flouride level**: 0.2 ppm

**Effects of Silica**

Silica, a naturally occurring mineral, plays a subtle yet significant role in our health, particularly when present in drinking water. Its influence ranges from potential benefits to specific harms, depending on various factors such as concentration and individual health conditions. Here, we explore these effects with a blend of scientific insight and a forward-thinking perspective.

**Benefits of Silica in Drinking Water:**

- **Enhanced Bone Health:** Research suggests silica contributes to bone formation and health, potentially reducing the risk of osteoporosis.
- **Improved Skin Elasticity:** Silica is believed to support skin health, promoting elasticity and reducing the appearance of fine lines.
- **Detoxification Properties:** It may aid in flushing out toxins from the body, supporting overall detoxification processes.
- **Supports Joint Health:** By aiding in the formation of collagen, silica can play a role in maintaining healthy joints.

**Potential Harms of Silica in Drinking Water:**

- **Kidney Stone Risk:** Excessive consumption of silica-rich water might increase the likelihood of developing kidney stones in susceptible individuals.
- **Digestive Issues:** In some cases, high levels of silica can lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or other digestive issues.
- **Possible Interactions:** Silica might interact with certain medications, altering their effectiveness.
- **Quality Concerns:** The presence of silica in water can indicate contamination with other, potentially harmful, minerals or substances.

In understanding the impact of silica in drinking water, it's crucial to maintain a balanced view. While it offers several health benefits, awareness of its potential risks is equally important for informed consumption.

**Effects of Calcium**

Calcium, an essential mineral, is not just a building block for bones and teeth; its presence in drinking water offers a fascinating intersection of health and hydration. This exploration delves into the nuanced ways calcium impacts our health when consumed through water, highlighting both the advantages and the lesser-known downsides.

**Benefits of Calcium in Drinking Water:**

- **Bone and Teeth Strengthening:** Calcium is pivotal in maintaining bone density and health, reducing the risk of osteoporosis.
- **Cardiovascular Health:** Adequate calcium intake helps in regulating heart rhythms and blood pressure.
- **Muscle Function:** It plays a key role in muscle contraction, including the proper functioning of the heart muscle.
- **Nerve Signaling:** Calcium is essential for the transmission of nerve signals in the body, supporting overall neurological health.

**Potential Harms of Calcium in Drinking Water:**

- **Risk of Kidney Stones:** Excessive calcium, especially when combined with other minerals like oxalate, can contribute to the formation of kidney stones.
- **Digestive Issues:** In some individuals, high levels of calcium can lead to constipation or other digestive problems.
- **Interference with Mineral Absorption:** High calcium levels can interfere with the absorption of other minerals like iron and zinc.
- **Hard Water Concerns:** While not directly harmful, high calcium content can contribute to water hardness, which may lead to minor inconveniences like limescale buildup in plumbing.

While calcium in drinking water is largely beneficial, understanding its full impact requires a consideration of both its health benefits and potential downsides. This balanced approach allows for a more informed and holistic view of our water consumption.

**Effects of Magnesium**

Magnesium, often overshadowed by other minerals, is a silent powerhouse in our diet, especially when it comes from a source as everyday as drinking water. This critical nutrient, while less discussed, plays a vital role in numerous bodily functions. Here, we delve into the effects of magnesium when consumed through water, highlighting its benefits and potential drawbacks in a balanced and insightful manner.

**Benefits of Magnesium in Drinking Water:**

- **Cardiovascular Health:** Magnesium aids in maintaining a healthy heart rhythm and is linked to reduced risks of hypertension.
- **Bone Health:** It plays a crucial role in bone density, complementing calcium for stronger bones.
- **Metabolic Function:** Magnesium is essential for numerous biochemical reactions, including energy production and glucose control.
- **Muscle Function:** It helps in muscle relaxation and reduces the likelihood of cramps and spasms.
- **Mental Well-being:** Adequate magnesium intake is associated with reduced stress and anxiety levels, promoting overall mental health.

**Potential Harms of Magnesium in Drinking Water:**

- **Laxative Effect:** High levels of magnesium, particularly in certain forms, can have a laxative effect, leading to digestive discomfort.
- **Kidney Strain:** For individuals with kidney issues, excessive magnesium intake might exacerbate their condition.
- **Imbalance of Minerals:** An unusually high magnesium level can disrupt the balance of other minerals in the body, like calcium and potassium.
- **Interaction with Medications:** Magnesium can interact with various medications, altering their absorption and effectiveness.

The role of magnesium in our health, especially through drinking water, is a testament to the intricate balance of nutrients our bodies require. Understanding this balance helps us appreciate the subtle yet powerful impact of what we consume daily.

**Effects of Bicarbonates**

Bicarbonates, often unnoticed constituents of drinking water, play a nuanced role in human health. They are more than just components of water; they represent a fascinating intersection of chemistry and well-being. In this exploration, we shed light on the subtle yet impactful effects of bicarbonates consumed through water, offering a balanced view that marries scientific understanding with a holistic health perspective.

**Benefits of Bicarbonates in Drinking Water:**

- **pH Balance:** Bicarbonates help maintain the body's pH balance, crucial for overall health.
- **Digestive Aid:** They can neutralize stomach acid, providing relief from indigestion and heartburn.
- **Metabolic Benefits:** Bicarbonates play a role in metabolic processes, aiding in the management of metabolic acidosis.
- **Enhanced Hydration:** They may improve hydration efficiency, especially during physical activity.

**Potential Harms of Bicarbonates in Drinking Water:**

- **Digestive Imbalance:** Excessive bicarbonate levels can lead to alkalosis, disrupting the stomach's natural acid balance.
- **Electrolyte Disturbance:** High bicarbonate intake can affect the body's electrolyte levels, potentially causing issues like hypokalemia.
- **Interactions with Medications:** Bicarbonates can interact with certain medications, affecting their absorption and efficacy.
- **Water Quality Concerns:** The presence of high bicarbonate levels can indicate underlying issues with water quality and purity.

The presence of bicarbonates in drinking water is a double-edged sword, offering both health benefits and potential risks. Understanding these effects empowers us to make informed decisions about our water consumption and overall health strategies.

**Effects of Dissolved Solids**

Dissolved solids in drinking water, an ensemble of various minerals and organic materials, present a complex and intriguing aspect of our daily hydration. These microscopic constituents, invisible to the naked eye, significantly influence the quality and health impact of the water we consume. In this insightful exploration, we unravel the nuanced effects of dissolved solids in water, embracing a scientific yet accessible approach to understanding their impact on human health.

**Benefits of Dissolved Solids in Drinking Water:**

- **Mineral Intake:** Essential minerals like calcium, magnesium, and potassium, often part of dissolved solids, are crucial for various bodily functions.
- **Taste Enhancement:** Moderate levels of dissolved solids can improve the taste of water, making it more palatable.
- **Hydration Efficiency:** Some minerals in dissolved solids can enhance the body's absorption of water.
- **Electrolyte Balance:** They contribute to maintaining the body's electrolyte balance, especially important for athletic performance.

**Potential Harms of Dissolved Solids in Drinking Water:**

- **Hard Water Issues:** High levels of dissolved solids can lead to hard water, which might have minor health implications and can cause scaling in pipes and appliances.
- **Mineral Imbalance:** Excessive intake of certain minerals through water can disrupt the body's mineral balance.
- **Potential Contaminants:** Some dissolved solids may include harmful substances like heavy metals, which can pose health risks.
- **Aesthetic Concerns:** High concentrations of dissolved solids can affect the appearance, taste, and smell of water, making it less appealing.

Understanding the role of dissolved solids in drinking water is crucial for a comprehensive appreciation of our hydration choices. While they offer essential benefits, it's important to be mindful of their potential drawbacks for a well-rounded approach to water consumption.
